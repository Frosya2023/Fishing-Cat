All about fishing cats

Appearance

The fishing cat is a medium-sized wild cat found in South and Southeast Asia.
It’s a threatened species that has seen a significant decline in population over the past decade.
Unlike other cats, the fishing cat spends most of its time in dense vegetation near water and is an excellent swimmer.
It has a cool gray-brown coat with black spots and stripes.
The cheeks have white highlights and black marks, and the eyes are ringed with white fur.

Diet

The fishing cat is known for its viverrine or civet-like appearance, but it doesn’t actually fish.
The name comes from its affinity for water and its ability to swim well.
It primarily feeds on fish, but it also eats other aquatic creatures like frogs, crabs, and even birds.
The fishing cat is an agile hunter that uses its sharp claws to catch prey.
It’s an creature that has adapted to its watery habitat and is an important part of the ecosystem
